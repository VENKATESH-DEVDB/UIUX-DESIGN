We set type by hand because the eye can tell the difference. Every job — a wedding suite, a chapbook, a single letterpressed card — gets the same attention a book does: chosen paper, mixed ink, a press bed adjusted until the impression is even across the whole sheet.

01 — The Craft

Three disciplines, one workshop.

Letterpress Printing

Invitations, stationery, and broadsides set from hand-composed metal and wood type, run on a Vandercook proof press.

Hand Bookbinding

Sewn-board journals, edition bindings, and single-copy restorations, cased in cloth, paper, or full leather.

Bespoke Stationery

Custom die lines, foil, and letterpress combined for wedding suites, correspondence cards, and studio identities.

02 — In the Bindery

How a book leaves this room.

  1. 01

    Fold

    Printed sheets are folded into signatures on the bone folder, grain running with the spine.

  2. 02

    Sew

    Signatures are sewn through the fold on linen thread, station by station, on a wooden sewing frame.

  3. 03

    Case

    The sewn block is rounded, backed, and cased into boards covered in cloth or paper.

  4. 04

    Press

    The finished book rests in a nipping press overnight so the case sets flat and true.
